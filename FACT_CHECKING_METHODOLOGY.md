### 2. CAMERA SPECIFICATIONS

#### Primary Sources
1. **Official Camera Specs**
   - Sensor model (e.g., "GN1 Optical Image Stabilization")
   - Aperture (e.g., "f/1.6")
   - Focal length (e.g., "24mm equivalent")
   - Sensor size (e.g., "1/1.56 inch")
   - Source: Official product sheet

2. **GSMArena Camera Database**
   - Comprehensive camera details
   - Sample photos
   - Detailed specs
   - Reliable for most specifications

3. **DXOMark** (for detailed camera testing)
   - Independent testing
   - Controlled environment
   - Comparative rankings
   - Website: dxomark.com

---

#### How to Verify Camera Claims

**Example: "50MP camera with f/1.6 aperture"**

Verification process:
```
1. Go to official Vivo website
2. Find product specifications
3. Check exact camera text
4. Compare with GSMArena
5. Note any differences
6. Use official specification
7. Document source
```

### 4. BATTERY & CHARGING SPECIFICATIONS

#### Primary Sources
1. **Official Battery Specs**
   - Capacity (mAh)
   - Type (Li-Po, Li-Ion, LiFePO4)
   - Charger wattage
   - Charging time (often tested, may vary)

2. **Regulatory Documents**
   - BIS certification (India) - lists battery mAh
   - FCC filings (USA) - detailed battery info
   - Battery safety certifications

3. **Manufacturer Testing**
   - Official charging time claims
   - Battery health retention %

---

#### How to Verify Battery Claims

**Example: "5000mAh battery, 80W charging, ~28 minutes to 100%"**

Verification:
```
Step 1: Check official spec
- Go to: Vivo.com/in product page
- Note: Battery capacity stated
- Note: Charger wattage stated
- Note: Any charging time claims

Step 2: Cross-check GSMArena
- Compare battery capacity
- Compare charger wattage
- Note: Charging time rarely listed (varies)

Step 3: Real-world testing
- Charging time varies by:
  - Charger quality/age
  - Phone temperature
  - Battery health
- Don't claim 100% accuracy
- Use range: "28-32 minutes"
```

**Important:** Charging times are highly variable. Use:
- Official claim + "in optimal conditions"
- Your testing + "in our testing"
- Range, not exact number: "25-30 minutes"

### 5. DISPLAY SPECIFICATIONS

#### Primary Sources
1. **Official Display Specs**
   - Size (diagonal, in inches)
   - Type (AMOLED, IPS, etc.)
   - Resolution
   - Refresh rate
   - Brightness (nits)

2. **Brightness Verification**
   - ⚠️ Different measurement methods exist
   - Peak brightness vs sustained brightness
   - HDR brightness vs SDR brightness
   - Best source: GSMArena (standardized testing)

3. **Certification**
   - Color gamut (sRGB, DCI-P3)
   - Touch response time
   - Sometimes available from manufacturers

---

#### How to Verify Display Claims

**Example: "1400 nits peak brightness AMOLED display"**

```
Step 1: Verify official spec
- Check: Vivo official product page
- Look for: "Peak brightness: 1400 nits"
- Note: Some brands don't publish nits

Step 2: Cross-check GSMArena
- GSMArena tests brightness standardized
- Compare: Vivo vs competitors at GSMArena
- Note: May differ slightly from manufacturer claim

Step 3: Understand context
- Peak brightness = short burst (good for sunlight)
- Sustained brightness = longer duration
- Both metrics matter for real use
- Clarify in your article which is claimed
```

### 6. 5G & CONNECTIVITY SPECIFICATIONS

#### Primary Sources
1. **Manufacturer Specifications**
   - 5G bands supported (n78, n79, n1, etc.)
   - Wi-Fi standard (Wi-Fi 6E, etc.)
   - Bluetooth version

2. **Telecom Operators** (For India)
   - Jio: Supports bands n78
   - Airtel: Supports bands n78, n79
   - Vi: Band information

3. **FCC Database** (USA standards)
   - Lists all spectrum bands
   - Official certification

---

#### How to Verify 5G Claims

**Example: "Supports Jio 5G and Airtel 5G"**

```
Step 1: Check manufacturer specs
- Go to: Vivo official page
- Look for: "5G Bands: n78, n79, n1, n3..."
- Note: Exact band numbers

Step 2: Verify with Indian telecom
- Jio 5G: Primarily uses n78 band
- Airtel 5G: Uses n78 and n79 bands
- Vi 5G: Different band usage

Step 3: Confirm compatibility
- If phone has n78: ✅ Compatible with Jio & Airtel
- If phone has n79: ✅ Compatible with Airtel
- If phone missing bands: ⚠️ May have limited 5G

Step 4: Test in real world
- 5G availability varies by location
- Test in major cities (Bengaluru, Delhi, Mumbai)
- Document: Location, network, speeds
```

## ⚠️ RED FLAGS - STOP & VERIFY

If you're unsure about any claim, STOP and verify:

### 🚩 RED FLAG 1: Claim without source
**Don't write:** "This phone has the best camera"
**Instead verify:** "According to DXOMark, ranking is..."

### 🚩 RED FLAG 2: Exact number without source
**Don't write:** "Reaches 90°C during gaming"
**Instead write:** "In our testing: reached 45-48°C"

### 🚩 RED FLAG 3: Comparison without both specs
**Don't write:** "Better than Samsung S23"
**Instead write:** "vs Samsung S23: wins on battery, loses on zoom"

### 🚩 RED FLAG 4: Update promise without source
**Don't write:** "Gets Android updates until 2029"
**Instead write:** "Vivo promises 3 years of OS updates"

### 🚩 RED FLAG 5: Feature claims without verification
**Don't write:** "Latest chipset available"
**Instead write:** "Snapdragon 8 Elite (released Dec 2024)"
