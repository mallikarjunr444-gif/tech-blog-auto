## ❌ WORDS/PHRASES TO ELIMINATE

### AI-Pattern Phrases (Immediate Ban)
- ❌ "proof of the phone's excellent"
- ❌ "it's a great option for"
- ❌ "without any issues"
- ❌ "seamlessly"
- ❌ "cutting-edge"
- ❌ "as expected"
- ❌ "no deal-breaker"
- ❌ "genuinely good" (overuse)
- ❌ "you won't be disappointed"
- ❌ "a perfect choice for"

### Weak Descriptors (Replace with Specifics)
- ❌ "excellent" → ✅ "achieved X benchmark" or "outperformed Y in Z"
- ❌ "good" → ✅ "provides 1.5 days battery life"
- ❌ "great" → ✅ "reaches 1400 nits brightness"
- ❌ "nice" → ✅ Show actual usage example
- ❌ "very" → Delete this word entirely

### Vague Testing Claims (Add Numbers Instead)
- ❌ "works well" → ✅ "maintained 90fps for 45 minutes straight"
- ❌ "performs excellently" → ✅ "scored 1.4M in AnTuTu"
- ❌ "handles it great" → ✅ "experienced 0-30% in 10 minutes"
- ❌ "improves battery life" → ✅ "extends battery by 4 hours vs Galaxy S23"

## ✅ PHRASES TO USE INSTEAD

### Specificity Language
Replace vague with precise:

| Generic | Specific | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| "Good performance" | Actual benchmark | "Scored 1.4M in AnTuTu, 2,400+ in GB6" |
| "Nice display" | Specific specs + testing | "1400 nits tested in direct Bengaluru sun" |
| "Great battery" | Timeline + usage | "15 hours real usage: gaming+work+social" |
| "Good camera" | Real photo examples | "Indian skin tones rendered naturally vs oversaturated competitors" |

### Honest Language
- ✅ "This isn't the best camera in the world"
- ✅ "Wireless charging is a glaring omission at ₹84,999"
- ✅ "OnePlus wins on raw performance—marginally faster"
- ✅ "The iPhone 15 Pro is noticeably better in low light"

### Personal Authority Language
- ✅ "After 14 days of testing, here's what I found..."
- ✅ "In real usage, this 5000mAh battery lasts..."
- ✅ "I tested this against OnePlus 11 Pro and Galaxy S23..."
- ✅ "When I dropped it in a puddle, here's what happened..."

## 📝 STRUCTURE TEMPLATE FOR PHONE REVIEWS

### Minimum Sections (Follow This Order)

1. **Title + Meta Description** (SEO-optimized)
2. **First Impressions** (Real unboxing experience, not specs)
3. **What Makes It Special** (3 key differentiators, not just specs)
4. **Testing Methodology** (Transparent, reproducible)
5. **Complete Specs** (Table format, linked to explanations)
6. **Design & Build** (Material quality, comfort testing, durability)
7. **Display** (Brightness tested, color accuracy, real-world usage)
8. **Performance** (Benchmarks + real-world gaming/work)
9. **Gaming** (FPS locked, temperatures, specific games)
10. **Camera** (Real locations tested, comparison vs rivals)
11. **Battery** (Timeline + specific usage breakdown)
12. **Software** (UI experience, bloatware, update promise)
13. **Connectivity** (5G speeds tested, Wi-Fi 6E, NFC)
14. **Comparisons** (Head-to-head vs 3+ rivals, specific wins/losses)
15. **Pricing & Variants** (Which to buy, effective prices)
16. **Pros & Cons** (Honest, with explanations)
17. **Who Should Buy** (Audience segmentation)
18. **Final Verdict** (Rating, recommendation, use-case guidance)
19. **FAQ** (10+ detailed Q&A)

**Minimum word count:** 8,000 words

## 🎬 REAL-WORLD TESTING CHECKLIST

Every phone review must include actual testing data:

### Battery Testing
- [ ] Full 24-hour usage timeline with actual percentages
- [ ] Real mixed usage (gaming, work, social media)
- [ ] Charging speed tested with stopwatch
- [ ] Temperature during charging monitored
- [ ] Multiple charging cycles tested
- [ ] Calculation: Hours of usage per 1% battery

### Gaming Testing
- [ ] 3+ different games tested (BGMI, Genshin Impact, FC Mobile)
- [ ] Specific settings used (max graphics, FPS locked, etc.)
- [ ] Temperature readings at 5, 15, 30, 45, 60-minute marks
- [ ] FPS consistency checked (drops noted)
- [ ] Thermal throttling observed (or not)
- [ ] Comparison vs specific rival phones

### Camera Testing
- [ ] 3+ locations tested (daylight, low light, indoors)
- [ ] Tested against 2+ rival phones with same scenes
- [ ] Specific test subjects: landscapes, portraits, food, text
- [ ] Indian skin tone rendering tested
- [ ] Zoom quality tested at 1x, 2x, 4x, 10x magnification
- [ ] Night mode compared vs rivals

### Display Testing
- [ ] Brightness tested in actual sunlight (specific nits)
- [ ] Color accuracy vs specific rival phones
- [ ] Refresh rate consistency during gaming
- [ ] Eye comfort tested (duration mentioned)
- [ ] HDR content tested (Netflix, specific shows)

### Performance Testing
- [ ] App switching tested (does it reload apps?)
- [ ] Gaming benchmarks (AnTuTu, Geekbench) with actual scores
- [ ] Real-world tasks timed (app installs, file transfers)
- [ ] Multitasking tested (specific number of apps)
- [ ] Storage speeds tested (read/write benchmarks)

## 💬 TONE GUIDELINES

### ✅ DO:
- **Be conversational:** "I went in skeptical..." vs "This phone is revolutionary..."
- **Admit limitations:** "No wireless charging is a glaring omission"
- **Compare honestly:** "iPhone is better, here's specifically why"
- **Use personal examples:** "After 45 minutes of BGMI, temperature hit..."
- **Acknowledge complexity:** "There's no perfect phone, here's the tradeoff"

### ❌ DON'T:
- **Sound robotic:** Avoid repetitive structure and AI-pattern phrases
- **Make unsupported claims:** Always back with numbers
- **Praise blindly:** Every product has weaknesses
- **Use hyperbole:** "Best phone ever" loses credibility
- **Sound like ads:** If you sound like the brand, readers distrust you

### 🎯 TARGET TONE:
**Knowledgeable friend who tested this phone extensively and wants to save you money.**

## 🎓 EXAMPLES OF QUALITY WRITING

### ❌ AI-LIKE (Don't Do This)
"The vivo X300 FE is a great phone with excellent performance and good battery life. It's a perfect choice for those who want a flagship experience. The camera is good and the display is excellent. This phone is definitely worth your money."

**Why it fails:**
- Generic descriptors (great, excellent, good)
- No specific examples
- Sounds like marketing copy
- No personal testing
- No comparison
- No audience segmentation

### ✅ EXPERT-LIKE (Do This)
"I tested this phone for 14 days. Here's what you need to know: The battery lasts 15 hours with real mixed usage (gaming, work, social media). The camera renders Indian skin tones naturally—not oversaturated like the Galaxy S23. Gaming maintains 90fps with no throttling. OnePlus 11 Pro is slightly faster, but costs ₹5,000 more. If battery and camera matter, pick Vivo. If raw performance matters, pick OnePlus. The 12GB/256GB variant at ₹74,999 is the best value."

**Why it works:**
- Specific data (15 hours, 90fps, ₹5,000 difference)
- Personal testing (14 days, real usage)
- Honest comparisons (acknowledges rival strengths)
- Real audience segmentation (which buyer should choose which)
- No AI-pattern phrases
- Clear recommendation with reasoning
